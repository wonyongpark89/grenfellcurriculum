The Project
Our Approach
A rigorous, community-centred methodology that puts lived experience at the heart of curriculum design.

Listen & Learn

We begin by listening to the Grenfell community — survivors, bereaved families, and local residents — to understand their experiences and priorities for what young people should learn.

Research & Develop

Working with academic partners and curriculum experts, we develop evidence-based resources aligned with national curriculum standards and pedagogical best practice.

Co-Create & Test

Every resource is co-created with teachers and community members, then piloted in schools to gather feedback and refine content before wider release.

Train & Support

We provide CPD training and ongoing support for educators, ensuring they feel confident and prepared to deliver sensitive and impactful lessons.

Evaluate & Iterate

Independent evaluation tracks the impact of our resources on student understanding, empathy, and civic engagement, feeding back into continuous improvement.

Share & Scale

All resources are made freely available online, with tailored support for schools in diverse contexts — from inner-city London to rural communities nationwide.

Guiding Principles
What grounds our work
Four principles guide every decision we make — from the first community conversation to the finished resource.
Participation

Nothing About Us Without Us

The community is not a subject to be studied — they are co-authors. Meaningful participation at every stage is non-negotiable.

Rigour

Evidence Over Assumption

Every resource is grounded in academic research, Inquiry findings, and teacher expertise. We hold ourselves to the highest educational standards.

Sensitivity

Trauma-Informed Practice

We approach difficult content with care, providing frameworks for teachers to handle sensitive topics with confidence and empathy.

Access

Free for Every School

Cost should never be a barrier. All resources are free, adaptable, and designed to work in any school context or setting.
