Demographic Profile

Which statement best describes the demographic profile of Grenfell Tower residents before 2017?

  • A largely homogeneous group of long-term British residents
  • Mostly single professionals working in finance and law
  • A diverse, multicultural community across ages, professions, and nationalities
  • Primarily recent refugees with no long-term residents
Suggested discussion / feedback

Grenfell Tower was home to a highly diverse community before the fire. Residents came from many different countries and ethnic backgrounds, represented a wide range of ages and life stages, and worked in varied professions. Some had lived in the tower for decades, while others were more recent arrivals. This diversity challenges common misconceptions that Grenfell residents were a single, uniform group and helps underline that the disaster affected a complex, established community rather than an anonymous or marginalised population.

Concerns Raised

Which concerns about safety were raised by Grenfell residents before the fire?

  • Flammable cladding materials
  • Lack of sprinklers
  • Absence of a building-wide fire alarm system
  • Blocked or unsafe exits
Suggested discussion / feedback

Before the fire, residents raised multiple, specific concerns about safety in Grenfell Tower. These included worries about the flammability of the newly installed cladding, the absence of basic fire safety measures such as sprinklers and a building-wide fire alarm, and problems with exits and evacuation routes. Importantly, these were not vague or isolated complaints: they were repeated, detailed, and grounded in residents’ lived experience of the building. The failure to respond meaningfully to these warnings was a critical factor in the disaster.

Life before the Fire

In one or two sentences, explain why understanding life before the fire matters.

Suggested discussion / feedback

Understanding life before the fire is important because Grenfell was a lived-in community, not just a site of tragedy. Focusing only on the night of the fire risks overlooking the everyday relationships, histories, and forms of care that existed within the tower, as well as the longer-term social, political, and housing conditions that shaped residents’ lives. Seeing Grenfell in this broader context helps explain why the disaster had such profound human and social consequences.
