CPD Week 3

The Future of Grenfell (2)

Answer these reflection questions on the future of Grenfell.

Question

Why has criminal justice taken so long to materialise for the Grenfell community, and what tensions exist between thoroughness of investigation and the human need for timely accountability?

Suggested discussion / feedback

The criminal investigation has been exceptionally complex, involving over 460 companies, 31 million documents, and potential charges ranging from corporate manslaughter to fraud. Police needed to wait for the inquiry's Phase 2 report (published September 2024) before completing their files for the CPS. While thoroughness increases the chance of successful prosecution, the near-decade wait has caused immense distress to bereaved families — encapsulated in the phrase "justice delayed is justice denied." This tension highlights how the legal system can fail to meet the emotional and moral needs of victims even when it functions as intended.

Question

What does the decision to demolish Grenfell Tower, rather than preserve it, reveal about the challenges of memorialising a tragedy while also supporting a living community?

Suggested discussion / feedback

The demolition decision reflects a difficult balance. Engineering reports confirmed the tower was deteriorating and posed safety risks, making preservation impractical. For some, the tower was a sacred connection to lost loved ones; for others, it was a source of daily trauma. The choice to demolish and replace it with a community-designed memorial acknowledges that memorialisation must serve the living community — offering a space for peace, reflection, and remembrance — rather than preserving a painful ruin. It also shows how memorials must evolve through genuine community participation, not top-down decisions.
