The Project
About Us
Born from tragedy, driven by purpose — building a curriculum that transforms grief into lasting social change.

The Grenfell Curriculum is an educational initiative that develops teaching resources inspired by the lessons of the Grenfell Tower fire of 14 June 2017 — a tragedy that claimed 72 lives and exposed deep systemic failures in housing, regulation, and civic accountability.

We work with educators, survivors, bereaved families, and community organisations to create age-appropriate, cross-curricular materials that bring these vital lessons into classrooms across the United Kingdom and beyond.

Our mission is to ensure that future generations understand not only what happened at Grenfell, but why it happened — and what each of us can do to build a more just and accountable society.

  • ✦

    Truth & Accountability

    Centring the voices of those most affected and holding power to account through education.

  • ◈

    Community-Led

    Every resource is co-developed with the Grenfell community to ensure authenticity and respect.

  • ◇

    Cross-Curricular

    Materials designed for citizenship, PSHE, English, history, science, and more.

  • ○

    Accessible to All

    Free resources for every school, adaptable across key stages and learning needs.

Our Journey
Key Milestones
From the first conversations with the community to classrooms across the country.
June 2017

The Grenfell Tower Fire

72 lives lost. The tragedy exposes systemic failures in housing safety, regulation, and accountability.

2019

First Community Conversations

Listening sessions with survivors, bereaved families, and educators to shape the vision for a curriculum response.

2021

Project Launch

The Grenfell Curriculum is formally established with academic and community partners.

2023

First Resources Published

KS3 and KS4 teaching packs launched, piloted in 50 schools across London and beyond.

2025

National Reach

Over 200 schools engaged. New KS2 and KS5 resources launched. CPD training programme established.
